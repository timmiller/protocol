Chapter 4

Clinical Flow

The event-day sequence from patient arrival to administration. Whether you're running a dinner seminar group treatment day or a single-patient clinic visit, this flow keeps everything organized, documented, and compliant.

Patient Arrival & Intake

  1. Confirm hydration. Ask the patient if they drank 64 oz or more of water in the past 24 hours. If not, have them drink water now and give it 15–20 minutes before attempting IV access. Reschedule IV portion if severely dehydrated.

  2. Review the treatment plan. Confirm which joints are being treated and whether IV is included. Cross-reference with the patient's purchase/consent documentation.

  3. Collect signed consent. All patients must sign informed consent before any administration. Paper consent is preferred — most patients in this demographic are more comfortable with a physical document they can review at home. Have consent ready before any product is touched.

  4. Brief patient interview / assessment. Ask about current pain levels (0–10), location, duration, any changes since initial consultation. Palpate joints being treated. Note findings on intake sheet.

  5. Confirm no contraindications. Active infection at injection site, fever, or acute illness → reschedule. Review current medications — blood thinners do not contraindicate treatment but are noted. Active cancer or immune-suppressive conditions → consult supervising physician.

Pre-Injection Checklist

✅
Run through this checklist before any product is opened. It takes 2 minutes and prevents every costly error in this manual.

Clinical Readiness

  • ☐ Consent signed and filed
  • ☐ Treatment plan confirmed (which joints + IV or joint-only)
  • ☐ Injection sites palpated, landmarks identified
  • ☐ Supplies laid out and counted (syringes, needles, gauze, prep pads, gloves)
  • ☐ IV established and confirmed patent (if IV is in the plan)
  • ☐ Injection sheet ready to document during or immediately after

Product Readiness

  • ☐ Correct product pulled for this patient's protocol
  • ☐ Lot number noted on injection sheet
  • ☐ COA on file for this lot
  • ☐ Product still frozen — do not thaw until IV access is confirmed
  • ☐ BioAer (if used) — not yet reconstituted; reconstitute immediately before use
  • ☐ Saline drawn up for IV push vehicle and flush

Administration Sequence

Recommended Order

When a patient is receiving both joint injections and IV on the same day, follow this sequence:

  1. Establish IV access first while cells are still frozen.
  2. Thaw IV product (BioFlow) while you perform joint injections.
  3. Administer joint injections (BioFlex / BioFusion) — product for joints is drawn from separate thawed vials.
  4. Confirm IV product is ready (fully thawed, no ice).
  5. Administer IV push and flush. See Chapter 2 for full IV protocol.

This sequence maximizes cell viability — the IV cells thaw during the time spent on joint injections, minimizing the wait after thawing.

NP Training Framework

Three-Phase Onboarding

For NPs new to this system, we recommend a three-phase approach regardless of the training format used — whether that's in-person observation, video review of procedures, or a combination of both:

Phase Format Goal
Phase 1 Observation (in-person or video) Review full procedures, documentation, and patient interaction before administering independently
Phase 2 Supervised practice NP performs procedures with clinical support available — in person or on a live call
Phase 3 Independent operation NP operates independently; Regenerative Revival clinical team available by phone or text for questions

Scheduling a group of 4–6 patients for Phase 2 is ideal — it exposes the NP to a variety of injection sites and scenarios in a single session. Your specific training path will be confirmed during onboarding.
