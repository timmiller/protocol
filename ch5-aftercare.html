Chapter 5

Aftercare & Follow-Up

The first two weeks after treatment are the most critical for cell integration. What a patient does — and doesn't do — in this window significantly affects outcomes. Discharge instructions, activity guidance, diet, and your follow-up touchpoints all live here.

Immediate Post-Treatment (Same Day)

Before the Patient Leaves

  • Patient remains seated for 5–10 minutes post-IV before standing
  • Assess injection site(s) — any immediate swelling, redness, or unusual discomfort
  • Some discomfort at the injection site is normal and should resolve within 15–20 minutes as the fluid distributes
  • Provide printed discharge instructions — do not send them home without a physical copy
  • Confirm follow-up contact method — phone number, preferred time
  • Answer questions before they leave

Activity Restrictions

⚠️
Counterintuitive but important: The instinct is to move and exercise to promote healing. For stem cell therapy, the opposite applies immediately post-treatment. Strenuous activity disperses the cells before they've had time to establish at the injection site.
Timeframe Activity Level Notes
Days 1–2 Rest / light movement only Walking to car, bathroom, around the house is fine. No exercise.
Days 3–7 Light daily activity Grocery store, short walks. No running, no gym, no sports.
Weeks 2–4 Gradual increase Begin light walking program. No heavy weights, no high-impact sports.
After Week 4 Return to activity as tolerated Listen to the body. No major golfing, running, or heavy gym lifting until 4+ weeks out.
💡
The first two weeks of cell activity are the most important. Cells released into a joint that is immediately stressed through heavy exercise may migrate away before integrating. Patience in weeks 1–2 leads to better outcomes at 90 days.

Anti-Inflammatory Diet Guidance

Why It Matters

Research supports better stem cell outcomes in patients who follow an anti-inflammatory eating pattern. This is not a short-term prescription — the longer patients maintain this approach, the better their results tend to be. Frame it as a lifestyle recommendation that supports and extends their investment.

Encourage More Of

  • Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) — omega-3 rich
  • Leafy greens (spinach, kale, arugula)
  • Berries (blueberries, cherries, strawberries)
  • Olive oil (primary cooking fat)
  • Nuts and seeds (walnuts, flaxseed)
  • Turmeric, ginger — natural anti-inflammatories
  • Water — maintain hydration continuously post-treatment

Reduce or Eliminate

  • Refined sugar and high-fructose corn syrup
  • Processed and packaged foods
  • Trans fats and refined vegetable oils (canola, soybean)
  • Refined carbohydrates (white bread, white rice, pastries)
  • Alcohol — especially the first two weeks
  • Red and processed meats (in excess)
💡
A modified Mediterranean diet or anti-inflammatory ketogenic approach aligns well with these guidelines. Patients familiar with keto or paleo eating are already most of the way there.

Follow-Up Protocol

ℹ️
Manage expectations: Patients should not expect to notice significant changes until the 60–90 day mark. Some report changes earlier; some later. Cases have been documented where patients felt results at 8–9 months. Set this expectation clearly at discharge.
Touchpoint Method Purpose
Day 7 Phone check-in Assess for any adverse events, answer questions, confirm they're following aftercare
Day 30 Phone or in-person Early progress check — note any changes, reassure those who haven't felt results yet
Day 60 Phone or in-person Mid-cycle assessment — most patients beginning to notice something by now
Day 90 In-person (recommended) Full outcome assessment; pain scale comparison; photography if applicable; discuss next treatment if warranted
Month 6 In-person Repeat treatment decision point — Seth's protocol. Some practices wait 12 months.

Repeat Treatment Timing

Two schools of thought exist on when to repeat:

  • 6-month repeat: Seth Berge's current practice. Captures patients still in an improvement arc.
  • 12-month repeat: Used by some practices who believe cells continue working for up to 9–12 months post-treatment.

In practice, roughly 15% of patients come back for a repeat treatment at their own initiative. Most who initially didn't respond significantly will consider a repeat with the benefit of a different product combination or additional exosome support.

Adverse Event Protocol

What to Watch For

Event Frequency Response
Temporary increase in pain / soreness at injection site Common Expected. Reassure patient — this is the body responding. Usually resolves in 1–3 days.
Flu-like symptoms (fatigue, low-grade ache, mild fever) Occasional "Healing crisis" — the body processing inflammatory signals released by new cell activity. Typically resolves within a few days. Encourage rest and hydration.
Injection site infection (redness, warmth, pus, fever) Rare (<1%) Contact patient. If signs of infection: refer to urgent care or prescribing physician immediately. Document and report to Precision Biologics.
IV infiltration (swelling outside the vein) Occasional Remove IV immediately. Apply ice first, then heat. Do not re-inject product that extravasated.
Vasovagal (fainting/near-fainting) Rare Have patient lie down immediately. Apply cool cloth. Monitor vitals. Delay or abort IV if patient doesn't stabilize quickly.
🚨
Any severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis), chest pain, shortness of breath, or neurological symptoms — call 911 immediately. These events are exceedingly rare but stem cell therapy involves a biological product. Know your location's emergency response protocol before each treatment day.

Discharge Instructions (Patient Copy)

What to Include in the Printed Handout

  • Activity restrictions (by week)
  • Anti-inflammatory diet overview
  • What's normal to feel in the first 48–72 hours
  • Signs to watch for and call the clinic
  • Follow-up schedule and contact number
  • Reminder: results are expected at 60–90 days, not immediately
